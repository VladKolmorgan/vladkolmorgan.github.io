HELLDIVERS The Game

HELLDIVERS 2 is a 3rd person shooter in which you and up to 3 other players are launched at extremely fast speeds into the surface of an alien infested planet with one simple goal: liberate and spread managed democracy. You are always outnumbered, but that doesn’t mean you are outgunned. Well, usually you will be, but HELLDIVERS has a unique system to equal the playing field called stratagems. These are various equipment and ordinance which you will use to make a swarm of enemies into a smoldering pile of liberty! The goal is to liberate all occupied planets or planets being sieged to ensure democracy is spread across the whole galaxy.
The game map for players is a map that displays the galaxy that they reside in. At the center is the capital Super Earth, and all around are Super Earth controlled planets, past that is enemy occupied planets. These planets are the goal, to liberate every single planet from their alien counterparts so Super Earth can exploit the planets for resources. Every mission completed adds onto a liberation counter for a planet, once it reaches 100% the planet is completely liberated. Liberation of planets takes tens of thousands of players, sometimes even hundreds of thousands of people trying to contain alien threats.

Helldivers And Super Earth

Super Earth is a significantly more advanced version of Earth, which is ruled by a certain set of ideals. Prosperity, liberty, and democracy are the only way of life the citizens of Super Earth know. Any other form of government, or even insults to the holding government, are looked on as grievous crimes and are usually punished by freedom camps or even capital punishment.
However, within Super Earth’s government and military is a branch of the military called the Helldivers. Helldivers are regarded as an elite peacekeeping force for Super Earth. Their purpose is to deal with any threat to Super Earth’s government policies. This can vary from traitors to aliens, the main enemy of the game. Helldivers are dropped into the worst possible situation deep into enemy territory to complete a set of objectives. Once all objectives are complete the Helldiver squad of 4 members are advised to extract, however that may not always be possible. In these cases their brave sacrifices will never be forgotten nor will they have been in vain.

Weapons Gear

Helldivers, the players, will be almost always outnumbered, and with enemy units who are tanks who can block bullets there needs to be ways to deal with these threats. Each helldiver gets a primary which can be a one of many things (machine guns, snipers, shotguns, etc) they also get a sidearm (pistol, submachine gun, lasers) and a grenade of their choice (stuns, explosives, fire). However, as strong as the weapons can be it isn’t enough for the extra strong enemies, or more often, the swarms of enemies that attack you. That is where stratagems come in. Players can select 4 different stratagems before a mission, each player by default gets a team ammo drop, a reinforce for respawning dead teammates, and an SOS beacon to ask for help from other players looking for a match. Stratagems vary from equipment, to vehicles, to orbital ordinance, to close air support, to even defenses such as turrets.
A single helldiver that utilizes their stratagems well can remove hundreds of enemies including. Ultimately though, even if they are armed to the teeth they are still one person, and a person with only so much skill and ammo. That is why players must play with their team to win, and if they lose their team they need to revive them. This helps to promote team play and interaction between players.
Stratagems use certain button combinations in order to use. For example, in a mission while on a controller if you hold the L1 on a Playstation controller, it will open a panel that shows all the stratagems you can call in and their cooldowns. Then, hypothetically, a player wants to revive their teammate, they would press their Dpad on the left of their controller up, down, left, right, up. Then, all they need to do is throw it, or drop it if they get knocked down, to use the stratagem. However, there are dozens of stratagems to accommodate several different play styles.

The Automatons

The automatons are an alien species which are simply robots. They come in a variety of enemies from foot soldiers with chainsaws for arms, to foot soldiers with rocket launchers, to massive hulking masses of metal which are as thick as a tank, oh and they have tanks too. The Automatons are considered hard, but there is a lot of debate if they are harder than the second enemy faction or not. Their goal is simply the destruction of Super Earth, as Automatons are an offshoot of the Cyborgs, a faction from the first HELLDIVERS, with a minor change being that they simply don’t have flesh.
Automatons are focused on firepower and will release countless rounds at the Helldivers in order to remove them from their territory and repel the invaders. They also have rockets, tank shells, artillery, landmines, and even lasers at their disposal. These make the Automatons a truly powerful foe that cannot be underestimated.

The Terminids

The second, and likely most popular faction in Helldivers, are the Terminids. These large bugs are reminiscent of the bugs from the movie STARSHIP TROOPERS, in fact the whole game is vaguely similar to that movie. However, these bugs are very important to Super Earth, as they can be turned into a very rich fuel for their spaceships. It is actually due to this that the Terminids were used in farms. Sadly, they broke free and are now rampaging the galaxy as a bug swarm that destroys any human they see.
Funnily enough, the Terminids are considered undemocratic and deserving of liberty by Helldivers according to Super Earth. As you can imagine the Terminids mostly consist of bugs, mainly bugs that resemble that of ants, roly poly, fleas/mites, or even a mantis. The most lethal without a doubt is a bug called a hunter or the larger version the stalker (who can go invisible), who functions as a large mantis that can cut down a Helldiver in only a couple slashes, despite bullet proof armor. There are also bile spewers that spit out acid onto their enemies, flying bugs, and even a giant bug that towers over the battlefield that can easily crush friend and foe alike.

Other Factions

Currently there are only two factions, the Automatons and the Terminids, however in the first HELLDIVERS there was a third. A squid like creature the Illuminate were an advanced civilization, however their government was seen as undemocratic and was deemed worthy of liberation, as well as their advanced technologies of course. It is highly likely if not guaranteed that they will make their appearance at some point in the game's lifetime. The devs are likely waiting for a good time to release them upon Super Earth to have Helldivers fight this threat.
The Illuminate are a very weak faction and due to this need to use shields and cloaking to protect or hide themselves. In the first HELLDIVERS they use lasers and electricity to subdue and eliminate their enemies. They were also notorious for their ability to stun targets, disorientate, and even teleport themselves. If, if not when, the Illuminate are added to the game players will likely find themselves struggling against such opponents.
It is possible an unknown fourth faction will appear, but at this time events in the game occur in which unknown blue lasers hit players on mission. It is safe to say the next enemy that will be introduced to the galactic war is the Illuminate.
